What I Believe

In these tumultuous times, I believe it is more important than ever for people of conscience to stand in their values and share them openly. While I understand that my being upfront about my values may turn away some potential clients, for others it signals safety and the opportunity for collaboration based on trust, shared values, and mutual respect.

In contrast to performative politics that only address the symptoms that arise out of oppressive systems while leaving them in place, I seek to embody a radical politics, which means getting to the root of systemic issues. In order to do so, we need to be able to identify and name the harms of capitalism, militarism, colonization, white supremacy, ableism, and patriarchy.

We need to be aware of and care about the ways these abusive powers kill people and the planet, as in the ongoing genocides in Sudan, the Congo, and Palestine, and industry's stripping of the earth's precious and finite resources in the global south. The earth is one living organism and we are so much more connected than we have been taught to believe.

I believe in an equitable and just world. I believe in land back and indigenous sovereignty, from Hawai'i to Palestine and all over the globe. I believe in abolition and reparations. I believe that the authentic expression of all queer identities should be celebrated and protected. I believe in a world where people of all abilities live safely and securely.

I believe that we all have a part to play in creating this world and that the more of us who find the courage to align ourselves fully with our values, the closer we are to bringing it into reality.
